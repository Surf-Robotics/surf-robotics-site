The Challenge

2026–27 FTC Game

This season's FTC game challenges teams to score game pieces in elevated goal structures, navigate a dynamic field, and complete end-game tasks requiring precise positioning and coordination with alliance partners.

Our strategy focuses on a fully-automated 30-second autonomous period and a fast, repeatable teleop scoring cycle using our custom linear slide mechanism and vision-assisted targeting.

Our Robot

SURF BOT MK IV

Drivetrain

Mecanum Wheel

4-wheel holonomic drive for full omnidirectional movement. Powered by 4× REV HD Hex motors.

Scoring Mechanism

Dual Linear Slides

Custom-machined 3-stage linear slides with belt-driven extension. Max reach: 36 inches.

Intake

Compliant Wheel Roller

Active intake with adjustable servo-controlled pivot for picking up game pieces from multiple orientations.

Vision

OpenCV + AprilTags

Logitech C920 with custom OpenCV pipeline for AprilTag field localization and target detection.

Control System

REV Control Hub

REV Control + Expansion Hub running FTC SDK 9.x with custom road-runner integration.

Weight

17.8 lbs

Within FTC 18 kg limit. Chassis: aluminum extrusion. Panels: polycarb. Hardware: titanium bolts.

How We Got Here

Design Process

01

Kickoff & Game Analysis

September 2026

Watched the game reveal, broke down scoring opportunities, and held a 3-day strategy sprint with the full team to decide our robot concept.

02

Prototyping

October 2026

Built 3 drivetrain prototypes and 2 intake concepts using cardboard and PLA. Tested with game pieces before committing to final design.

03

CAD & Fabrication

November 2026

Lily completed full Onshape model. Ryan ran 3 weeks of CNC and lathe work to produce all custom parts for the drive base and slides.

04

Software Integration

December 2026

James and Priya integrated Road Runner for autonomous path following. All game piece detection pipeline complete and tested.

05

Qualifier → Regionals → (fingers crossed) State Championships. We compete, iterate, and compete again.
