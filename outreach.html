Why It Matters

Giving Back to Our Community

For us, robotics isn't just about winning competitions. It's about inspiring the next generation of engineers and makers in our community. Every outreach event, workshop, and mentoring session is as important as any match we compete in.

Since 2022, we've logged over 500 hours of community outreach — reaching students from kindergarten through high school across 12+ events per year.

Our Programs

What We Do

🏫

Classroom Visits

We visit elementary and middle school classrooms with our demo robot, running interactive Q&A sessions and hands-on coding activities using basic robotics kits.

10+ schools per year
🔧

FLL Team Mentorship

We mentor three local FIRST LEGO League (FLL) teams, helping younger students through their own engineering design process and competition preparation.

3 active FLL teams
🌊

Coastal Kids STEM Day

Our flagship annual event brings 100+ elementary students to experience hands-on STEM activities, robot demos, and career talks from local engineers.

Annual · May
👩‍💻

Girls in STEM Workshop

A biannual coding and robotics workshop specifically designed for middle-school girls, addressing the gender gap in STEM fields through role models and hands-on experience.

Biannual
📡

Library Maker Series

Monthly maker workshops at our local public library covering topics from soldering to 3D printing to basic electronics — free and open to all ages.

Monthly · All ages
🏆

FTC Demo Days

We bring our competition robot to community events like farmers markets, school fairs, and local festivals to spark curiosity and recruit future team members.

Various events
